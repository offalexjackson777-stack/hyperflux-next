# Code Of Conduct

## Our Standard

HyperFlux Next welcomes careful technical disagreement, new contributors, and
questions asked in good faith. Participants are expected to be respectful,
specific, patient, and honest about evidence and uncertainty.

Acceptable participation includes:

- criticizing ideas and code without attacking people;
- distinguishing observation, inference, qualification, and unknowns;
- protecting private or security-sensitive information;
- making space for different experience levels and communication styles;
- correcting mistakes without humiliation or retaliation.

Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, doxxing,
deliberate privacy violations, sustained disruption, and bad-faith manipulation
of evidence are not acceptable.

## Scope And Enforcement

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, Discussions, Projects,
security coordination, and other spaces where someone represents the project.
Maintainers may edit or remove content, lock conversations, reject
contributions, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when
needed to protect the community or the project.

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository's
[security advisory intake](https://github.com/offalexjackson777-stack/hyperflux-next/security/advisories/new)
and mark the report as a conduct concern. Do not include unrelated private
information. Maintainers will handle reports as confidentially as practical and
will avoid conflicts of interest where possible.

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant while using project-specific
language for a small, evidence-driven hardware and systems community.
